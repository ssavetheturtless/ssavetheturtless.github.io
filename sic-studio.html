is an experimental design and research studio
we sit at the intersection of international Development, the digital space, and economics
While we are optimistic about the potential of society and our relationship with the natural world, we are critically realistic about the way things are.

We acknowledge the imbalance of power in current development work.

This comes with the acknowledgment that we don't know where the best solutions may come from. So, we look to empower the local communities who understand these opportunities best.
We are local stakeholders led in identifying problems and envisioning solutions.

We are founded on the idea of giving more than just a voice, but a say.  And ‘development’ doesn’t equate to ‘growth.’ Instead, the local community defines their future as we help where we can. This support is founded on values of longevity and regeneration.

Our research focuses on impact measurement and intervention efficacy.

For design, our work puts experiments into practice to test hypotheses, then we take these learnings to develop solutions, while also informing futher and future research endeavors. It's a virtuous cycle!

Our Projects

Karen Virtual Museum

What does it mean to preserve an identity and culture online for an ethnic group living a makeshift life?

Developed with my students organically in a class I taught on active citizenship, this project looks to preserve and present the Karen culture and traditions. We explore the value a the digital space offers a forcibly displaced and scattered ethnic group as well as its limits on creating a community.
Through this project we look to learn about overcoming the digital divide, the furthest extents of the digital domain, and what it can offer communities marginalized in their own local and domestic physical spaces.

Khee Harvest

Can development efforts of 'unbalanced growth' be led by the local community and entrepreneurs for more sustainable development?

We are a direct-trade coffee company. Our goal is to send the value back to the origin—the farmer—where much of the labor, time, and craft is consolidated. We are looking at the effects of economic empowerment on local political, social, and economic stability.
Economic empowerment is explored through the opening of the global market to farmers by advances in communications technologies and the Internet. We are looking to continue to innovate by testing certain web3 protocols in our processes and how this may help further empower farmers living in conflict zones and unstable, oppressive environments.
